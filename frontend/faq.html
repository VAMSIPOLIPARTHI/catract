Find answers to common questions about our AI technology and process.

help What is a cataract?

A cataract is a clouding of the typically clear lens of your eye. For people who have cataracts, seeing through cloudy lenses is a bit like looking through a frosty or fogged-up window.

medical_information Does this replace a doctor?

Absolutely not. CataractDetect AI is a preliminary screening tool. It provides a risk assessment based on imagery, but only a qualified ophthalmologist can provide a clinical diagnosis and treatment plan.

lock Is my data private?

Yes. We prioritize your privacy. Uploaded images are processed in-memory for the duration of the request and are not permanently stored in any database. The downloadable report is generated on the fly.

model_training How does the AI work?

We utilize a technique called Transfer Learning. We have fine-tuned two powerful convolutional neural networks (ResNet50 and VGG16) on a specific dataset of categorized eye images (Normal, Cataract, Glaucoma, etc.) to learn the visual features of cataracts.

warning Can the AI make mistakes?

Yes. Like any software, it can produce false positives or false negatives, especially if the image quality is poor, lighting is bad, or there is glare. Always consult a professional for definitive advice.
